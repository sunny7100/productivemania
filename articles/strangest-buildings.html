Strangest Buildings in the World

6 min read July 2024

Architecture has always pushed boundaries, but these buildings take creativity to extraordinary levels, challenging our perception of what structures can be.

The Crooked House, Poland

Located in Sopot, this building looks like it's melting or from a fairy tale. Inspired by Polish fairy tale illustrations, the Crooked House serves as a shopping center and has become one of Poland's most photographed buildings.

Habitat 67, Canada

This Montreal housing complex looks like a giant pile of concrete blocks stacked randomly. Designed for Expo 67, each unit has its own rooftop garden, creating a unique three-dimensional community that defies conventional apartment design.

The Basket Building, USA

The headquarters of the Longaberger Company in Ohio is shaped like a giant picnic basket, complete with handles. This seven-story building is 160 times larger than the company's actual baskets and weighs about 9,000 tons.

Atomium, Belgium

Built for the 1958 World's Fair in Brussels, this structure represents an iron crystal magnified 165 billion times. Standing 335 feet tall, visitors can travel through tubes connecting the spheres and enjoy panoramic views of Brussels.

Cubic Houses, Netherlands

Designed by Piet Blom in Rotterdam, these houses are tilted 45 degrees and rest on hexagonal poles. The concept was to create a forest of houses where each house represents a tree, and together they form a village.

The Upside-Down House, Poland

This house in Szymbark is built completely upside-down, from the roof to the interior furnishings. It was designed to make a statement about the state of the world and Communist-era Poland, taking 114 days to build due to worker disorientation.

Architecture Beyond Imagination

These extraordinary buildings prove that architecture can be both functional and fantastical, pushing the boundaries of design and engineering.
